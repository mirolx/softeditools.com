How to Convert Units — A Simple Guide for Everyday Conversions

May 20, 2026 · SoftEdit Tools

Unit conversion sits quietly at the edge of dozens of everyday situations: a recipe in cups when your measuring jug shows millilitres, a road sign in miles when you think in kilometres, a parcel weight listed in pounds when your scales read kilograms. Most people manage by rough guesswork — but guesswork fails when precision matters, and even when it does not, a quick accurate conversion takes almost no effort once you know how.

Why Unit Conversion Comes Up More Than You Think

The world uses two broad measurement systems that never fully merged. Most countries follow the metric system — metres, kilograms, litres, Celsius — built around powers of ten and designed to be simple to scale. The United States, along with a small handful of other countries, still uses the imperial system for everyday life — miles, pounds, gallons, Fahrenheit — a collection of units with historical roots but less mathematical elegance.

As a result, anyone who travels internationally, shops from foreign websites, follows recipes from other countries, or works in science, medicine, engineering, or construction will encounter the need to convert regularly. Even within a single country, different industries favour different units: a nutritionist works in grams and kilojoules; a builder works in feet and inches; a pharmacist measures in milligrams and millilitres.

Knowing a handful of key conversion factors — and understanding how to apply them — removes the friction from all of these situations.

The Most Common Conversions and Their Formulas

The majority of everyday conversions fall into four categories: length, weight, volume, and temperature. Here are the formulas you will reach for most often:

km → miles: multiply by 0.621
miles → km: multiply by 1.609
kg → lbs: multiply by 2.205
lbs → kg: multiply by 0.454
litres → US gallons: multiply by 0.264
cm → inches: multiply by 0.394

Temperature is the exception — it requires a formula rather than a simple multiplication factor, because the two scales start at different zero points. To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit, multiply by 9/5 and add 32. To go the other way, subtract 32 and multiply by 5/9.

For length and weight, a useful mental shortcut: a kilometre is roughly five-eighths of a mile; a kilogram is just over two pounds. These approximations are accurate enough for navigation and everyday shopping without any arithmetic.

To reverse any of these, simply divide by the same factor — or equivalently, multiply by its reciprocal. Miles to kilometres: multiply by 1.609. Inches to centimetres: multiply by 2.54. The relationship is always symmetrical.

How to Convert Units Manually

Manual conversion is straightforward once you have the right factor. The method is always the same: identify what you are converting from and to, find the conversion factor, and multiply (or divide, depending on direction).

For example, to convert 5 kilometres to miles: 5 × 0.621 = 3.1 miles. To convert 150 pounds to kilograms: 150 × 0.454 = 68.1 kg. To convert 30 cm to inches: 30 × 0.394 = 11.8 inches. Each calculation takes seconds and requires nothing more than basic multiplication.

Where manual conversion gets tedious is when you need many conversions in a row, when the numbers are unwieldy, or when you are working with less common unit pairs — fluid ounces to millilitres, acres to square metres, knots to kilometres per hour. Memorising a conversion factor for every possible pair is neither practical nor necessary.
