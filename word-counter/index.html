Type or paste your text directly into the box above. Every statistic — word count, character count (with and without spaces), sentence count, paragraph count, and estimated reading time — updates in real time as you type. No button to click, no page refresh needed.

Frequently asked questions

How do I count words online?

Just paste or type your text into the box at the top of this page. The word count updates instantly — no need to click anything. This free word counter works in every modern browser on both desktop and mobile devices.

Is this word counter free?

Yes, completely free. There is no sign-up, no account, and no usage limit. You can count words online as many times as you like at no cost, forever.

Does this tool count characters?

Yes. You will see two character counts displayed side by side: one that includes spaces, and one that excludes them. Both update in real time alongside your word count, sentences, and paragraphs.

What is a good word count for an essay?

It depends on the assignment. High school essays are typically 500–800 words. College application essays usually fall between 250 and 650 words. Academic papers and undergraduate essays often range from 1,500 to 5,000 words, while graduate theses can exceed 10,000 words. Always follow the specific guidelines provided by your instructor.

How many words is a 5 minute read?

At an average silent reading speed of 200–250 words per minute, a 5-minute read is roughly 1,000–1,250 words. The reading time shown by this tool is calculated at 200 words per minute, rounded up to the nearest minute, which is a common conservative estimate used across publishing and content platforms.

Does punctuation count as a character?

Yes. In the "Characters" count, every character — including punctuation marks such as commas, periods, question marks, and quotation marks — is counted. The "No Spaces" count excludes whitespace characters (spaces, tabs, line breaks) but still includes all punctuation.
