Frequently asked questions

What counts as a character?

Any single symbol in your text counts as a character — letters, digits, punctuation marks, symbols, and spaces. The "Characters" count on this page includes every one of them. The "No Spaces" count is the same but with all whitespace (spaces, tabs, line breaks) removed.

Is this character counter free?

Yes, completely free. There is no sign-up, no account, and no usage limit. You can count characters online as many times as you like at no cost, forever.

Does it count spaces as characters?

Yes — the main "Characters" stat counts spaces as characters, just like most platforms do (Twitter/X, Instagram, SMS). If you need the count without spaces, use the "No Spaces" stat shown right beside it.

What is the character limit for Twitter/X?

Standard Twitter/X posts allow up to 280 characters, including spaces and punctuation. Verified users (Twitter Blue / X Premium) may have access to longer posts up to 25,000 characters, depending on their subscription tier.

What is the character limit for Instagram captions?

Instagram captions support up to 2,200 characters. However, only the first 125 characters are shown before the "more" link, so aim to put your most important message within the first 125 characters to maximise visibility in the feed.

How many characters are in a page?

A standard manuscript page (double-spaced, 12pt Times New Roman, 1-inch margins) holds roughly 1,800–2,000 characters including spaces, or about 250 words. A single-spaced page can hold approximately 3,000–3,500 characters. These figures vary depending on font size, line spacing, and margins.
